Seville: A City Forged by Time Seville is a city shaped by centuries of history, legends, and conquests. Believed to be founded by Hercules or the Turdetani tribe, it passed through the hands of Phoenicians, Greeks, Carthaginians, and Romans—who named it Iulia Romula. After the fall of Rome, Visigoths and later Muslims took over, renaming it Isbiliyya. The Almohad period saw Seville flourish, with iconic structures like the Giralda and Torre del Oro. Conquered in 1248 by King Ferdinand III, the city became a key Castilian capital. The 16th century brought architectural splendor, though economic decline followed. In the 19th and 20th centuries, Seville endured revolts, war, and disaster, but rose again with the 1992 World Expo. Today, it stands proud as Andalusia’s capital—vibrant, historical, and ever-evolving.
